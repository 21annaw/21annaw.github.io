What did I create, and why?

The spatial question I am trying to answer with this map is: how many residences (houses and apartments) are within 1 to 5 minute walking distances from banks? More specifically, I want to know the distribution of the residences within these different isochrome circles/layers.

I used the isochrome layers in order to reflect how many residences (houses and apartments) were contained within each extended layer of time frame. I used the counting feature and then added up the total, first for each timeframe, then for each bank’s surrounding layers respectively.

I chose this method because my goal was to figure out how exactly residences were distributed between different time frames. I believe that without labels, this would be a rather difficult (if not impossible) map to read, so therefore I added additional labels and information, in order to create a more interactive map.

Analysis of final vector map

From my map, a few key points become clear.

First of all, the banks are clustered together around the city center, especially surrounding Utrecht Centraal (our train station). Five out of the six isochromes overlap with each other, indicating a hotspot.

From the count analysis, we can conclude a few facts. When adding up all counts per timeframe, it results in: 1,202 houses and 933 apartments within a 5 minute walk of a bank; 59 houses and 43 apartments within a 2 minute walk of a bank; 6 houses and 9 apartments within a 1 minute walk of a bank.

Despite overlap occurring, since this consistent overlap can be attributed equally to all timeframes, the ratio will stay the same. There are numerous isochrome areas that have zero houses within a 1 or 2 minute walk of a bank. This may be partially due to the location of most of the banks being clustered directly around the train station, which contains mainly industrial/shopping buildings. This area of Utrecht is less residential and more aimed at transportation/shopping districts/businesses.

We can therefore conclude that there are more residences within a 5 minute walking distance than within a 1 or 2 minute walking distance.
